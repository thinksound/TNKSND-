Soundskrit (single directional element) — pros & cons

✓ Pros

  • Directivity for free — same first-order DI, formed acoustically.
  • WNG ≈ 0 dB — no amplification of uncorrelated noise.
  • Lower self-noise floor, especially at low frequency.
  • No capsule matching — robust and cheaper.
  • Flat on-axis response by design; only a mild LF self-noise rise (not perfectly flat).
  • Simpler, lower power.

✗ Cons

  • Fixed pattern — no electronic reconfiguration or steering.
  • Capped at first order (DI ≈ 4.8–6 dB).
  • No adaptivity to interferers.
  • Pattern/proximity vary with frequency & distance.

Bottom line: for a fixed first-order pattern the single element wins on noise & robustness; the array buys flexibility at the cost of low-frequency self-noise and matching.
